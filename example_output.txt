README:
The KEY shows how to read the output.  Each correction made by the algorithm is printed along with left and 
right context. Between the contexts is a 3-tuple in brackets consisting of: [true word,optimal candidate,OCR error]
If the algorithm corrected the OCR error then the true word == optimal candidate

Beneath each correction is the list of candidates that were considered as potential corrections beginning with the 
optimal candidate. Each candidate shows its associated log probabilities for the different elements of the model. 
[channel] is the weighted sum of [HMMchannel] and [HMMsource] (both operating at substring level)
and [lang] is the word level trigram language model. [total] is weights sum of all components where we are summing 
because we are in log probability space. 

[maxPartition] shows the sequence of most probable substring mappings where each tuple is of the form
(error_substring,candidate_substring) and you should think of it in terms of a noisy channel so that the 
noisy channel transforms the true word into ORC word via the sequence of substring mappings. For example
if the true word is 'John' and the OCR error is 'iuhn' then a typical mapping might be
[(i,j),(u,o),(hn,hn)]

The accuracy is just the standard (num correct predictions)/(num examples)
